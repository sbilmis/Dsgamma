\paragraph{Experimental status.}
The \(B_c^+\gamma\) mass spectrum contains an observed \(B_c(1P)^+\)-like
structure, with a two-peak description around \(6704.8\) and
\(6752.4~\mathrm{MeV}\) in the LHCb analysis.  The partial radiative widths are
not measured, so the experimental column should be read as an observation
status rather than a numerical width comparison.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The \(B_c\gamma\) channels are the present controlled prediction.  The quoted
first uncertainty is the Monte Carlo/input/Borel-window uncertainty of the
leading perturbative hard-QCDSR calculation, and the second is the conservative
\(G^2\) systematic envelope.  The \(B_c^\ast\gamma\) channels are quoted as
leading perturbative vector baselines.  The standard vector-invariant
normalization is preferred for the physical decay constant, while the reference
normalization shows the residual sensitivity to \(f_{B_c^\ast}\).  The
\(G^2\) screening envelope should be interpreted as a conservative systematic
size estimate pending the explicit vector contact-term calculation.
