# Stage 1: Tensor Basis For \(1^+\to1^-\gamma\)

The \(D_{s1}\to D_s^\ast\gamma\) calculation starts from

\[
\Pi_{\mu\nu}^{(i)}(p,q)
=i\int d^4x\,e^{ip\cdot x}
\langle \gamma(q,\varepsilon)|
T\{ \bar s(x)\gamma_\nu Q(x)\,J_{i\mu}^\dagger(0)\}
|0\rangle .
\]

The additional index \(\nu\) belongs to the final vector current.  This is the
main difference from the \(D_s\gamma\) calculation.

## Kinematics

\[
P=p+q,\qquad q^2=0,
\]

\[
\eta\cdot P=0,\qquad \xi\cdot p=0,\qquad \varepsilon\cdot q=0,
\]

where \(\eta\), \(\xi\), and \(\varepsilon\) are the polarizations of the
initial axial meson, final vector meson, and photon.

## Candidate Structures

Using the photon field strength

\[
F_{\alpha\beta}=q_\alpha\varepsilon_\beta-q_\beta\varepsilon_\alpha ,
\]

the first gauge-invariant even-sector structures are

\[
S_1=(\eta\cdot\varepsilon)(\xi\cdot q)
-(\eta\cdot q)(\xi\cdot\varepsilon),
\]

\[
S_2=(\eta\cdot q)\left[
(\xi\cdot\varepsilon)(P\cdot q)
-(\xi\cdot q)(P\cdot\varepsilon)
\right].
\]

A third apparent structure,

\[
S_3=(\xi\cdot q)\left[
(\eta\cdot\varepsilon)(p\cdot q)
-(\eta\cdot q)(p\cdot\varepsilon)
\right],
\]

is not independent after \(P=p+q\), \(q^2=0\), \(\varepsilon\cdot q=0\), and
\(\eta\cdot P=0\):

\[
S_3=(p\cdot q)S_1+S_2.
\]

We also keep the representative Levi-Civita structure

\[
S_\epsilon=\epsilon_{\alpha\beta\rho\sigma}
\eta^\alpha\xi^\beta\varepsilon^\rho q^\sigma
\]

as a diagnostic.  The FeynCalc traces will tell us whether the OPE naturally
projects onto the field-strength sector, the dual-field-strength sector, or a
linear combination depending on the current convention.

## Current Decision

For the first controlled baseline we should project the OPE onto \(S_1\) and
\(S_2\), while separately monitoring any epsilon-tensor output.  The decay
width should not be written in terms of a single form factor until we know
which structures survive numerically and how the helicity amplitudes combine.

## Polarization-Summed Width Matrix

For a general amplitude written as

\[
\mathcal M=c_1 S_1+c_2 S_2+c_\epsilon S_\epsilon,
\]

the decay width is

\[
\Gamma=
\frac{|\vec q\,|}{8\pi m_A^2}\,
\frac{1}{3}\sum_{\eta,\xi,\varepsilon}|\mathcal M|^2,
\qquad
|\vec q\,|=\frac{m_A^2-m_V^2}{2m_A}.
\]

The script

```bash
/Applications/Wolfram.app/Contents/MacOS/WolframKernel -noprompt \
  -script Dsstar_gamma/scripts/width_matrix_1p_to_1m_gamma.wl
```

uses explicit rest-frame polarization vectors and writes the matrix

```text
Dsstar_gamma/outputs/width_matrix_1p_to_1m_gamma.txt
```

such that

\[
\Gamma=
\frac{|\vec q\,|}{8\pi m_A^2}
\begin{pmatrix}c_1&c_2&c_\epsilon\end{pmatrix}
H
\begin{pmatrix}c_1\\ c_2\\ c_\epsilon\end{pmatrix}.
\]

The result is

\[
H=
\begin{pmatrix}
\dfrac{(m_A^2-m_V^2)^2(m_A^2+m_V^2)}{6m_A^2m_V^2}
&
-\dfrac{(m_A^2-m_V^2)^3}{12m_A^2}
&0\\[1.0em]
-\dfrac{(m_A^2-m_V^2)^3}{12m_A^2}
&
\dfrac{(m_A^2-m_V^2)^4}{24m_A^2}
&0\\[1.0em]
0&0&
\dfrac{(m_A^2-m_V^2)^2(m_A^2+m_V^2)}{6m_A^2m_V^2}
\end{pmatrix},
\]

with

\[
\frac{|\vec q\,|}{8\pi m_A^2}
=
\frac{m_A^2-m_V^2}{16\pi m_A^3}.
\]

Thus the even sector \((S_1,S_2)\) can interfere, but the representative
epsilon-sector structure \(S_\epsilon\) does not interfere with it after the
polarization sum.  The diagonal entries for \(S_1\) and \(S_\epsilon\) are the
same in this convention.
